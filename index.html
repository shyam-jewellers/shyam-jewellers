Baaju

Baaju jewelry, also known as arm jewelry or armlets, has been a cherished accessory in many cultures for centuries. It is typically worn around the upper arm and is often crafted from materials like gold, silver, or brass. Baaju jewelry comes in various designs, ranging from simple and elegant bands to intricate patterns studded with gemstones or engraved motifs

Bangels

Bangles are a timeless piece of jewelry that hold a special place in many cultures, particularly in South Asia. Crafted from materials like gold, silver, glass, or even wood, bangles come in a wide variety of designs, ranging from simple and sleek to heavily adorned with intricate carvings and gemstones.

Chokar

Choker jewelry is a type of necklace that sits snugly around the base of the neck, creating a bold and elegant look. Chokers have been a fashion staple across various cultures and eras, ranging from traditional designs made with precious metals and gemstones to contemporary styles crafted from lace, velvet, or leather.

Jhumka

Jhumka is a classic and timeless piece of jewelry that has been an integral part of traditional Indian accessories for centuries. These bell-shaped earrings are known for their distinctive design, featuring a dome or bell-like structure that often ends with a delicate cascade of tiny beads or pearls.

Mangalsutra

The Mangalsutra is a sacred necklace that holds profound cultural and spiritual significance in Indian tradition, symbolizing marriage and the eternal bond between a husband and wife. Typically made from gold, with a black beaded chain, the Mangalsutra is adorned with a pendant that may feature intricate designs, gemstones, or traditional motifs like the lotus, peacock, or simple geometric shapes.

Nath

Nath, also known as a nose ring or nose stud, is a traditional piece of jewelry that holds significant cultural importance in many Indian communities, particularly among brides. This ornate accessory is often worn on the left nostril and can range from simple designs to elaborate styles featuring intricate detailing, gemstones, and pearls. In Indian culture, the Nath is more than just an ornament; it symbolizes a woman's marital status and beauty.

Necklace

Necklace jewelry is a versatile and essential accessory that enhances the beauty and elegance of any outfit. Ranging from simple chains to intricate statement pieces, necklaces can be crafted from various materials, including gold, silver, gemstones, beads, and even fabric.

Payal

Payal, also known as anklets, are traditional pieces of jewelry worn around the ankles, holding a special place in many cultures, especially in India. These delicate adornments can be crafted from various materials, including silver, gold, and even beads, and often feature intricate designs that may include charms, bells, or gemstones.

Rakhdi

Rakhdi, also known as a maang tikka or head ornament, is a traditional piece of jewelry worn on the forehead, often adorning brides and women during special occasions and festivals in Indian culture. This exquisite accessory typically consists of a chain with a decorative pendant that rests on the center of the forehead, enhancing the beauty and elegance of the wearer.

Rings

Rings are one of the most versatile and cherished pieces of jewelry, symbolizing commitment, love, and personal style across cultures and eras. Ranging from simple bands to elaborate designs adorned with gemstones and intricate detailing, rings can be crafted from a variety of materials, including gold, silver, platinum, and even alternative metals like titanium.

Sui Dhaga

Sui Dhaga jewelry, also known as thread and needle jewelry, is a traditional adornment that holds significant cultural value in various Indian communities. This unique jewelry is characterized by the use of vibrant threads, often embellished with beads, sequins, and small charms, and is skillfully crafted to create intricate designs that reflect the rich heritage of handwork and artistry.

Tevta

Tevta jewelry, also known as Tehwar or Tewar, is a traditional form of adornment that holds cultural significance, particularly in certain regions of India. This intricate jewelry is characterized by its elaborate designs, often crafted from gold or silver and embellished with colorful gemstones, pearls, and intricate filigree work.
